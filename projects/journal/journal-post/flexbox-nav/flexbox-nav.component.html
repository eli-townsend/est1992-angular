Let’s break down what is happening behind the scenes for this menu system. We have a header that is containing our logo and our responsive menu hamburger icon. In a separate container #main-navigation that is displayed off the page, our menu links are housed. In stead of using display: none;, we hide the #main-navigation by setting the top position to top: -3000px; and the position to position: fixed; . To give the navigation the slide in transition, we set the transition to come in from the top at a half second. The easy solution to make the menu appear is we add the display property to the .open class. The .open class is now where we incorporate the flexbox layout.

If you have ever designed/developed a website, you know the frustrations of centering an element in the center vertically and horizontally on the page. Now with flexbox, it became very easy. With just this bit of code, you can accomplish the horizontal/vertical centering.
